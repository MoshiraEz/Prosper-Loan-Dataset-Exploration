Investigation Overview¶

This is a data analysis for investigating the borrower rate and its interrelationships with other variables.

Dataset Overview¶

We used a financial dataset from Prosper lending marketplace, it contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables, and stores information about Loans, Borrowers, Lenders, Interest rates and many other variables through the time from 2005 to 2014.

1- Tracking the change in the borrower rate over time¶

Firstly, we tracked the change in the borrower rate provided by Prosper market over years.

  • The line plot showed that the borrower rate dramatically grew up in 2005 and 2006, but slightly dropped in 2007. Then, it gradually increased through the years until it reached its highest in 2011. After that, it gradually dropped again through 2012 till 2014

2- Overview of the borrower rate interactions with other variables¶

Then, we created a heat map to explore the interactions between the borrower rate and other variables which are expected to influence the rate. The data revealed

  • Strong positive association with loan estimated return (r= 0.82), and estimated loss (r= 0.95)
  • Strong negative association with prosper score (r= -0.65)
  • Weak Negative association with the loan amount (r= -0.33), and the monthly loan payment (r= -0.24)
  • No association with stated monthly income (r=-0.089), and DIR (r= 0.063)

3- Effect of borrower income on the borrower rate¶

Next, we used the box plot to explore the effect of the borrower income range on the rate.

  • The box plot revealed that as the income increase, the interest rate decrease. Thus, not-employed borrowers recieve the highest interest rate.

5- Effect of loan term, and prosper score on the borrower rate¶

Afterwards, we created a line plot reflected the effect of the loan term and prosper score on the borrower rate. The line plot revealed that

  • The higher the length of the loan term, the higher the borrower rate.
  • The lower the prosper score (high risk), the higher the borrower rate (strengthing the negative correlation in the previous heat map).

6- Risk assessment of the high borrower rate loans¶

Surprisingly, we noticed in the previous overview heat map a strong positive correlation between the borrower rate and the estimated loan loss, where it was expected to show negative association, so we decided to draw a scatter plot exploring this relation according to the risk score.

  • Interestingly, the scatter plot conveyed that loans with high borrower rate and high estimated loss have high risk (i.e: low prosper score)
In [10]:
# plot a scatterplot  describing the relationship between borrower rate and estimated return and the prosper score
g = sb.FacetGrid(data = df, hue = 'ProsperScore', height= 5, aspect= 2)
g.map(plt.scatter, 'BorrowerRate', 'EstimatedLoss')
g.add_legend(title= 'Prosper Score')
plt.xlabel('Borrower Rate', fontsize= 16, weight= 'bold')
plt.ylabel('Estimated Loss', fontsize= 16, weight= 'bold')
